Bio

(Last updated: 2025)
I am a Master's student in Computer Science at the University of São Paulo (USP), where I am advised by João Neto. My research centers on large-scale data science applied to sectoral and economic analysis, with a particular focus on policy evaluation and learning.
In addition to my studies, I serve as a Research Assistant at Imperial College London under the supervision of Gabriel Lobato, PhD Candidate, as well as at FEA/USP under the supervision of Gilmar Masiero.
My academic background includes a BA in International Relations from ESPM/SP, featuring international programs at Zuyd University and Meiji University, alongside a BSc in Chemical Engineering from the University of São Paulo (USP). Professionally, my experience includes Quantitative Analysis at the China Construction Bank in Beijing. Moreover, I am the Founder of SMTech, a technology startup specializing in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science.
Beyond my academic pursuits, my interests lie in activities that foster strategic thinking. I am an avid Go player, which sharpens my foresight, and this appreciation for strategy extends to chess, a field in which I earned a scholastic championship. Furthermore, I practice boxing to cultivate self-control and planning skills. Finally, as a passionate Brazilian, I am a dedicated supporter of European football, with a particular allegiance to FC Barcelona.
