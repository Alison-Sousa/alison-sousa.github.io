Triangular Relationship Among the United States, China, and Taiwan in the Xi Jinping Administration: A Study on Country Tensions from the Perspective of Foreign Policy in Security, International Economy, and Spatial Econometrics
Alison Sousa, Alexandre Uehara
University of São Paulo, 2023
English, French, Portuguese

This paper investigates tensions among China, the United States, and Taiwan during Xi Jinping's governance, focusing on the period from 2012 to 2022. It aims to discern the drivers behind escalating tensions, analyzing security and economic dynamics among the nations. With tensions intensifying, particularly under Xi's leadership, China has sought to diplomatically and economically isolate Taiwan while diminishing US support. The research examines whether heightened tensions signal a potential Chinese offensive against Taiwan for national rejuvenation and explores the interplay between China's economic ties with the US and Sino-Taiwanese relations.

Legislative Process Analysis of Bill No. 5.660/2016 Regulating Internships in Brazil
Alison Sousa, Ananda Araujo, Fernanda Esteter, Paula Mesquita, Pedro Terras, Vitor Quadrado
Sigalei, 2022
Portuguese

This analysis examines Bill No. 5.660/2016, which aims to regulate internships across Brazil, ensuring enhanced protections and clearer guidelines for both interns and employers. The bill seeks to establish fair working conditions, define educational objectives, and align internship experiences more closely with academic curricula, thereby improving the overall internship framework within the country.
